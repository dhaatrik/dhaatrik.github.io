### The Spark of First-Principles Education

I have been teaching for roughly nine years. Over that time, I have guided around 200 students—ranging from crowded school classrooms of 30–40 students to offline coaching batches, 1:1 private lessons, and online sessions for NRI/international students across different time zones.

Throughout this journey, I realized that the conventional education system is obsessed with rote memorization. Students are trained to solve equations without ever understanding *why* the equations exist or what they represent in the physical world.

I started **DBS Classes** on YouTube on October 31, 2022, to break this cycle. The goal was simple:
1. Make Physics and Mathematics highly intuitive.
2. Teach using first-principles thinking.
3. Bridge the gap between abstract equations and real-life observations (like why a bus wheel rotates the way it does, or how kinematics play out on a football field).

### Unlocking the World of Math and Physics

The very first video, *Introduction: Unlocking the World of Mathematics and Physics*, was an invitation to look at science through a different lens. I wanted to build a repository of free, high-quality lessons that treated students not just as passive listeners, but as curious explorers.

Over the next few months, I poured my energy into creating structured lectures on trigonometry, dimensional analysis, and basic kinematics.
