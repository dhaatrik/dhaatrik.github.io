### The Spark of First-Principles Education

Dhaatrik has been teaching for roughly nine years. Over that time, he has guided around 200 students—ranging from crowded school classrooms of 30–40 students to offline coaching batches, 1:1 private lessons, and online sessions for NRI/international students across different time zones.

Throughout this journey, he realized that the conventional education system is obsessed with rote memorization. Students are trained to solve equations without ever understanding *why* the equations exist or what they represent in the physical world.

He started **DBS Classes** (short for *Dhaatu Bhaiyaa's Classes*, where "Dhaatu" is his personal nickname and "Bhaiyaa" is the Hindi word for big brother, reflecting what his students called him) on YouTube on October 31, 2022, to break this cycle, guided by a singular, non-negotiable philosophy:

<p align="center">
    <strong> "Education Is Not Free BUT KNOWLEDGE IS FREE" <br>
    ...and must be shared with "ZERO TERMS AND CONDITIONS"</strong>
</p>

With this core thought as his compass, the goal of the channel was straightforward:

1. Make Physics and Mathematics highly intuitive.
2. Teach using first-principles thinking.
3. Bridge the gap between abstract equations and real-life observations (like why a bus wheel rotates the way it does, or how kinematics play out on a football field).

### Unlocking the World of Math and Physics

The very first video, *Introduction: Unlocking the World of Mathematics and Physics*, was an invitation to look at science through a different lens. Dhaatrik wanted to build a repository of free, high-quality lessons that treated students not just as passive listeners, but as curious explorers.

Over the next few months, he poured his energy into creating structured lectures on trigonometry, dimensional analysis, and basic kinematics.
