### The Audit of a Failure

On September 15, 2023, I uploaded a video titled *Point Mass is a Myth*. It was my 20th educational video. And then, I stopped.

There was no grand external reason. No lack of equipment, no algorithm shadowban, no sudden crisis. The truth is much more boring and uncomfortable: **I stopped uploading because of my own flaws.**

I was inconsistent. I was unfocused. I was irregular.

The whole point of having a personal website is to document the journey honestly, which means owning the failures just as much as the successes. And I see the sudden end of the DBS Classes channel as a personal failure—one entirely of my own making.

### The Traps of Irregularity

When you launch an initiative like an educational channel, consistency is the only currency that matters. You owe it to the viewers, to the students, and to the goals you set for yourself to show up regularly. 

I fell into the classic traps:
* **The Novelty Fade:** The initial excitement of scripting and editing wore off, replaced by the friction of routine execution.
* **Loss of Focus:** I allowed myself to get distracted by other projects, telling myself I would "get back to it next week." Next week turned into next month, which turned into two years.
* **Lack of Discipline:** I relied on motivation rather than building a bulletproof system to produce content regularly.

### Embracing the Flaws

Acknowledging this failure isn't about self-pity; it is about self-calibration. If I cannot be honest about where I fell short—specifically my struggle with long-term execution consistency on solo side projects—I cannot hope to build robust systems in the future.

DBS Classes taught me how much I love teaching and how easily I can get distracted when there is no external accountability. It remains online as a snapshot of what was, and a reminder of the discipline I need to bring to everything I build next.
