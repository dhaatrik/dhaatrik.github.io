### The Audit of a Failure

On September 15, 2023, Dhaatrik uploaded a video titled *Point Mass is a Myth*. It was his 20th educational video. And then, he stopped.

There was no grand external reason. No lack of equipment, no algorithm shadowban, no sudden crisis. The truth is much more boring and uncomfortable: **he stopped uploading because of his own flaws.**

He was inconsistent. He was unfocused. He was irregular.

The whole point of having a personal website is to document the journey honestly, which means owning the failures just as much as the successes. And he sees the sudden end of the DBS Classes channel as a personal failure—one entirely of his own making.

### The Traps of Irregularity

When launching an initiative like an educational channel, consistency is the only currency that matters. A creator owes it to the viewers, the students, and the goals set for themselves to show up regularly. 

Dhaatrik fell into the classic traps:
* **The Novelty Fade:** The initial excitement of scripting and editing wore off, replaced by the friction of routine execution.
* **Loss of Focus:** He allowed himself to get distracted by other projects, telling himself he would "get back to it next week." Next week turned into next month, which turned into two years.
* **Lack of Discipline:** He relied on motivation rather than building a bulletproof system to produce content regularly.

```
[ October 31, 2022 ]  ===>  Launch (High Motivation)
        ||
        \/
[ Sep 15, 2023 - 20 Videos ] ===>  Friction & Novelty Fade
        ||
        \/
[ Post-Sep 15, 2023 ] ===>  Inconsistency & Cessation (Failure)
```

### Embracing the Flaws

Acknowledging this failure isn't about self-pity; it is about self-calibration. If he cannot be honest about where he fell short—specifically his struggle with long-term execution consistency on solo side projects—he cannot hope to build robust systems in the future.

The channel stood for a powerful ideal: **"Education Is Not Free BUT KNOWLEDGE IS FREE" and must be shared with "ZERO TERMS AND CONDITIONS"**. While Dhaatrik failed to sustain the channel, that core conviction remains fully intact and continues to shape his approach to building and sharing tools in his current engineering work.

DBS Classes taught Dhaatrik how much he loves teaching and how easily he can get distracted when there is no external accountability. It remains online as a snapshot of what was, and a reminder of the discipline he needs to bring to everything he builds next.
